## 2024-05-19 - Keyboard Accessibility and Focus Tracking
**Learning:** Adding keyboard shortcuts (like Escape to close overlays) greatly improves accessibility, but it's crucial to explicitly capture and restore focus (using \`document.activeElement\`) when these overlays open and close to maintain keyboard navigational flow and prevent focus from getting lost. Furthermore, existing visual keyboard shortcuts and structural icons must be marked \`aria-hidden="true"\` and paired with \`aria-keyshortcuts\` on their interactive parent button to prevent screen reader redundancy and confusion.
**Action:** Always save \`document.activeElement\` before displaying a full-screen or significant modal overlay, and restore focus to it upon dismissal. Audit all visual shortcut texts and decorative icons, ensuring they are hidden from the accessibility tree, moving their semantic meaning to ARIA attributes on the interactive element.
